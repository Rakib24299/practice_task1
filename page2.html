Our Story

A small studio on Galta Marg

Meherva began in 2019 as a quiet experiment. Our founder, Priya Mehra, wanted to create clothing that honored traditional Indian textile practices while serving contemporary wardrobes — pieces that could go from an afternoon in the atelier to a wedding reception without apology.

We work closely with artisan communities in Jaipur, Bombay, and Bangalore. Each piece is made to order, dyed using natural materials sourced from local farms, and finished by hand. The process is slow — deliberately so

Our collections are seasonal but timeless. We produce in small batches, never more than what we can oversee personally. This approach means we often sell out, but it also means we can maintain the quality and ethics that define our work.

"The best garments are those that improve with time — that carry stories in their fabric. We make slowly, because slowness is the only honest pace."

— Priya Mehra, Founder

Our practices

Natural materials

Every dye is plant-derived. Every fabric is organic cotton, silk, or a blend — certified and sourced directly from farms in Gujarat and Karnataka.

Fair wages

We pay artisans 40% above the industry standard and have maintained long-term partnerships since 2019.

Small batches

We produce only what we can oversee personally. If a piece sells out, we do not rush a reorder.

No waste

All garments are made to order. Fabric offcuts go back to artisan cooperatives for local use.
